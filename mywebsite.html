My Website

project duration: 3 months

I started designing and building this website right after I graduated from Web Design and Interactive Media, at Humber College in the Spring of 2020. The purpose of this website is to showcase my work, myself, and the skills I have as a multimedia designer. I wanted it to portray who I was while also displaying my artistic ability.

Style Guide

Before I even started thinking about my website's structure and design components, I focused on how I wanted it to look and feel. Because I wanted it to fully represent me, I took time to reflect. I asked friends for their opinions, thought about my music taste throughout the years, and contemplated on who I was as a person. After all the research I’d done, I decided on choosing a vibrant color scheme that I found matched my vibe. I have also always had a passion for 80's music and I wanted to incorporate that in my design. Lastly, I added some shadow colors to the color scheme that I can use for contrast to tie everything together.

When selecting typefaces, I chose Bebas Nue for my headings. Its bold and condensed look makes it great for differentiating sections. For body text, I chose Open Sans because it’s simply my favourite font; It’s easy to read and it pairs nicely with Bebas Nue.

Other Elements

After deciding which colors and fonts I wanted to work with, I went straight to creating basic wireframes. Next, I thought about some motion and graphic elements that I felt would bring my website to life. The graphic elements I used are the character piece in the introduction and the mail icon in the contact section. For motion, I used the animated bars, character, and flying mail animations.

The purpose of the animated bars are to help with navigation. They’re placed within each category to indicate how many sections there are left on the website. The last two elements, the animated character and flying mail, were used to provide balance and fill up excess space.

Execution

1. Wireframes

I first started off by sketching. Using a pencil and paper, I drew a basic layout of how I would like to organize my website and its content.


2. Mockups

After the wireframes were done, I went straight into Adobe Illustrator and started creating a polished version of how I wanted it to look with its style components.


3. Build

Once the mockups were done, I went straight into Visual Studio Code and started building my website. I started with developing the basic layout and styled them with CSS. I used filler text and colored div blocks to get an idea of how things would look before I used my actual content. When everything was in place, I made adjustments for responsiveness, added my javascript, and placed my content in. After I was done with the coding part, I started creating my animations, saving the best part for last. I imported my illustrator files into Adobe After Effects and went straight to work. After my animations compiled, I added them to my website and my project was finally completed.
